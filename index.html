Welcome to my webpage!

I am a doctoral candidate working with the Brain Dynamics and Control Research Group in the Preston M. Green Department of Electrical and Systems Engineering at Washington University in St. Louis. I am mentored by Dr. ShiNung Ching. The aim of my research is to understand the dynamics of neural coding with specific focus on olfactory coding. I use concepts from optimal control theory, machine learning, dynamical systems to implement these neural models. Interestingly, these models generate outcomes that are remarkably biological in nature.

Prior to this, I completed my undergraduate degree from Jadavpur University in 2015, and obtained my Masters degree from Washington University in St. Louis in 2018. My current research provides a complementary perspective to data-driven modeling approaches. I am passionate about learning new computational techniques that increases interpretability of data-driven approaches and fosters better understanding of biological systems.
